Design System
& Style Guide

A living design system proposal for DeepFlow's visual redesign — colour, type, nodes, edges, motion, and layout.

● Proposal v1.0 — March 2026
⚡
"Powerful enough for enterprise, friendly enough for first day."
Complexity exists to serve, never to impress. Every interaction should feel learnable within one session, while power users discover depth over months.
🎨
"Colour is data, not decoration."
Every fill, border, and gradient encodes information — depth level, status, or type. Eliminate decorative colour that doesn't carry meaning.
◇
"The graph is the hero — everything else supports it."
The workflow canvas is the flagship surface. It should be the most visually refined, most informative, and most engaging screen in the product.
✦
"Animation communicates life and progress."
Flowing edges, pulsing nodes, and smooth transitions tell users "the system is alive and working." Static diagrams feel dead. Orchestration platforms should feel orchestrated.
🔷
"Depth is colour — your position is always visible."
Blue is strategic. Pink is tactical. Your position in the hierarchy is encoded in every surface — nodes, badges, breadcrumbs, the sidebar. You always know where you are.
⬡
"Shape is identity — who does this is always clear."
Circles are human. Hexagons are AI. Two independent visual dimensions — shape for WHO, colour for WHERE — let you scan a graph and instantly understand the human/AI balance at any depth.

01 Colour System

Colour in DeepFlow is structural, not cosmetic. The depth gradient, status palette, and neutral ramp form three independent systems that layer without conflict.

Depth Gradient

The signature colour system. Hierarchical depth maps to a blue→red gradient. L1 (strategic) is cool cyan; L5+ (atomic tasks) is warm pink/red. Every node, badge, breadcrumb segment, and 3D overview layer reflects its depth.

ElementHow depth colour applies

✓ Do
Use depth colour to reinforce hierarchy. When a user drills from L1→L3, the entire canvas should shift from cool blue to warm purple — an ambient signal of depth.
Consistent gradient progression
✕ Don't
Never use depth colours for status or arbitrary decoration. A cyan node must mean L1 — not "active." Status has its own independent colour channel.
❌ Cyan border = "in progress" — conflicts with L1 depth meaning

Status Colours

Status colours communicate task state. They sit alongside depth colour without conflict — status appears as border glow, badge, or icon background; depth appears as fill tint.

Status encoding on nodes

EncodingUsage
Border glowPrimary status signal — a soft coloured glow surrounds the node
Badge iconSmall ✓ / ▶ / ⚠ / ✕ badge at top-right of node
Progress ring (circle nodes)For human tasks — a partial arc around the circle showing % complete
Edge colourDownstream edges from a failed node turn red-dashed
✓ Do
Double-encode status: colour + shape icon. This ensures accessibility for colour-blind users. A green checkmark, amber triangle, red cross.
✕ Don't
Never rely on colour alone for status. A red border without an icon or label is insufficient — ~8% of male users can't distinguish red from green.

Neutral Palette

A 10-step neutral ramp from near-black (sidebar) to near-white (canvas background). All greys are cool-toned with a subtle blue undertone to harmonise with the brand cyan.

04 Edges & Connections

Edge Types & Animated Flow

07 Workspace Layout

The three-panel workspace is where human-AI collaboration happens. Proportions, panel headers, and collapse affordances create a cohesive collaboration surface.

Three-Panel Proportions

Default split: 50% Workspace / 22% Chat / 28% Details. Panels are resizable with drag handles. Each panel is collapsible to a thin strip.
